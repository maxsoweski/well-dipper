Refactor Verification — Fixed-Timestep Phase 5 / AC #19 — Audio Clock Isolation

AC #19 of welldipper-fixed-timestep-migration-2026-05-03. Confirms fixed-step sim does not back-pressure the AudioContext clock. The audit-list ruling (docs/refactor-audits/fixed-timestep-migration-call-sites.md §Audio clock domain) documents zero sim->audio rate-pushing sites; this harness verifies the architectural property empirically by measuring audioContext.currentTime relative to performance.now() over a 60-second window while a synthetic looping tone is active and a simulated sim load occupies the JS main thread at > 50% utilisation.

Pass condition (qualitative, per brief AC #5 precedent): drift slope is bounded near zero and not monotone-growing across the window. Per-sample peaks are dominated by setInterval scheduling jitter (the JS event loop firing late under sim load) and do not represent audio-clock motion. A slope-based pass criterion is the right metric because audio drift induced by sim coupling would manifest as a sustained linear or accelerating divergence; per-sample noise cannot.

Pass thresholds: |slope| ≤ 1 ms/sec (over 60 s, projects to ≤ 60 ms accumulated end-to-end drift — comfortably under typical consumer audio device crystal variance of ~0.1 % / 100 ms per minute). End-to-end drift ≤ 10 ms. No monotone-growth signature in cumulative drift.
